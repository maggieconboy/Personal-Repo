How I Transform Dysfunctional Environments

Directors and VPs don't need heroes who save the day. They need systems that work when you're on vacation. I've spent my career walking into chaos—missed deadlines, political theater, zero visibility into decisions—and building repeatable frameworks that eliminate the dysfunction.

The Framework: Systems Over Heroics

At Texas Roadhouse, I inherited a contract on the verge of cancellation. Hotfix culture. No release discipline. Stakeholders demanding features with no prioritization. Within six months, we had fixed cadence, true ownership, and visible decision-making. The result? Multi-year renewal and 24+ months of zero missed deadlines.

At GitHub, I introduced the first agile methodology to IT Revenue Systems. Built trust across Salesforce, Zendesk, and revenue operations by delivering measurable automation and AI products. First PM to bring user research, journey mapping, and persona development to the team.

What Makes This Repeatable

The pattern is always the same: Diagnose the real problem (not what people say it is). Build operational rhythm through fixed cadence and rituals. Establish clear ownership and visible decisions. Ship incrementally and validate with real users. The dysfunction disappears because the system doesn't allow it to exist.
