Transformation Story

Joining GitHub to lead Revenue Systems meant navigating resistance, limited onboarding, and shifting priorities. Like my Texas Roadhouse experience, I started as an outsider and became an essential partner by delivering real value.

Q3 2025: Resistance

Observing the Barriers

Excluded from meetings and the original team dissolved. I shifted to program management and began mapping the core challenges by listening and documenting.

Response: Became a systems observer, identifying opportunities for change.
Q4 2025: Proof Point

Account Recommender Ships $6.3M

Took over Account Recommender when the team dissolved. Introduced sprint planning, story point estimation, and velocity tracking (all firsts for Revenue Systems). Shipped on time. Generated $6.3M in closed-won revenue.

Result: Revenue leaders started asking me to run other projects.
Q1 2026: Expansion

Scaling Agile and Automation

Extended agile practices to Zendesk and Salesforce teams. Launched effort-based planning, sprint ceremonies, and trade-off conversations, replacing date-driven commitments.

Impact: First successful agile sprints in Revenue Systems history.
Q2 2026: Partnership

Trusted Advisor

Now recognized as a strategic partner to Revenue Systems leadership. Piloting AI automation for reporting and issue refinement. Building bridges to the Product org for my next transition.

Next: Transitioning to Product team with leadership support.
