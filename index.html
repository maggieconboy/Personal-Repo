How I Build Scalable Systems

Most organizations operate on gut feel. I replace intuition with data, and heroic problem-solving with repeatable process.

The Problem

What I Kill to Ship

Most PMs can't articulate what they don't do. I eliminate three things immediately.

QA Theater

Situation: Product managers testing every ticket after dev and QA because no one trusts the process.

Action: Give QA true sign off authority and responsibility. If they approve it, it ships. Period. If QA can't own quality, fix the QA team.

Result: PMs focus on product decisions. QA owns quality. Zero time wasted on double-testing.

Documentation Debt

Situation: Manual docs and spreadsheets tracking what's already in your ticketing system (Jira, GitHub, etc). Two systems perpetually out of sync.

Action: Single source of truth in ticketing system. Give stakeholders access. Kill the docs and spreadsheets.

Result: Zero hours spent synchronizing systems. Real-time visibility for everyone who needs it.

Escalation Culture

Situation: Every misalignment triggers leadership meetings. Executives spending time managing operational decisions.

Action: Weekly sync with visible decisions. Right people in the room. No surprises.

Result: Course corrections happen in operational meetings. Escalations eliminated. Leadership focuses on strategy.
