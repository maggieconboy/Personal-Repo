Systems Over Heroics: My Operating Model

Directors and VPs don't need heroes who save the day. They need systems that work when you're on vacation. Here's the repeatable framework I deploy to make it happen reliably.

Phase 1

Inherited Chaos

Diagnose the Real Problem

  • Identify what's creating firefighting culture (usually trust gaps, not technical debt)
  • Map who actually makes decisions vs. who thinks they do
  • Find where documentation theater masks operational dysfunction
Phase 2

Tactical Systems

Build Rhythm and Visibility

  • Fixed release cadence: eliminates deadline negotiations, ships what's ready
  • True ownership: QA signs off on quality, clients and stakeholders UAT in system of record
  • Weekly decision sync: right people in room, decisions visible to everyone

Sustainable Impact

Eliminate Escalations Through Trust

  • Missed deadlines become a problem of the past
  • Misalignments become conversations, not crises requiring executive intervention
  • Teams operate independently because decisions are visible and rhythm is predictable

What I Kill to Ship

Most PMs can't articulate what they don't do. I eliminate three things immediately.

QA Theater

Situation: Product managers testing every ticket after dev and QA because no one trusts the process.

Action: Give QA true sign off authority and responsibility. If they approve it, it ships. Period. If QA can't own quality, fix the QA team.

Result: PMs focus on product decisions. QA owns quality. Zero time wasted on double-testing.

Documentation Debt

Situation: Manual docs and spreadsheets tracking what's already in your ticketing system (Jira, GitHub, etc). Two systems perpetually out of sync.

Action: Single source of truth in ticketing system. Give stakeholders access. Kill the docs and spreadsheets.

Result: Zero hours spent synchronizing systems. Real-time visibility for everyone who needs it.

Escalation Culture

Situation: Every misalignment triggers leadership meetings. Executives spending time managing operational decisions.

Action: Weekly sync with visible decisions. Right people in the room. No surprises.

Result: Course corrections happen in operational meetings. Escalations eliminated. Leadership focuses on strategy.
